# Per-Paper Imbalance Analysis

Data: `Microgravity_Database_Latest.csv`, 4,136 deduplicated labelled rows,
85 canonical papers. Full table: `audit_outputs/per_paper_stats.csv`;
figure: `audit_outputs/fig_paper_imbalance.png`. Generated by `fable_audit.py`.

## 1. Size imbalance

* Paper sizes span **3 to 314 rows** (median 30). Gini coefficient of the
  size distribution: **0.54**.
* The top 5 papers contribute **32%** of all rows; the top 13 contribute
  more than half:

| Paper | rows | share | ignition rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Konno 2023 (ISO 4589-4, firesaf.2023.103989) | 314 | 7.6% | 0.69 |
| Takahashi 2020 (Fire Technology) | 298 | 7.2% | 0.43 |
| Ma 2025 (firesaf.2024.104329) | 255 | 6.2% | 0.61 |
| Konno 2023 (proci.2022.07.002) | 241 | 5.8% | 0.67 |
| Urban 2025 (ICES-2025-392) | 221 | 5.3% | 0.48 |

* Under unweighted training, the gradient contribution of one paper's
  idiosyncrasies (rig, protocol, label conventions) is proportional to its
  row count: the Konno wire campaigns alone (4 papers) steer ~17% of the
  loss. This is the statistical justification for paper-balanced weighting
  (requirement A): rows within a paper are **not** independent draws from
  the population of microgravity-combustion experiments; papers are.

## 2. Class-composition imbalance (the more dangerous one)

* **40 of 85 papers (47%) contain only one class — and all 40 are
  100%-ignition papers (903 rows, 21.8% of the data).**
* **Zero papers are 100%-no-ignition.** Every no-ignition row in the corpus
  lives inside one of the 45 mixed papers.
* This is a *reporting artefact of the literature*, not physics: flame-spread
  studies routinely report only the conditions at which they obtained a
  flame; failed ignitions are mentioned in passing or not tabulated at all.

Consequences:

1. **Class imbalance (76/24) is largely a paper-composition effect.** The
   mixed papers alone are 71/29. Global `scale_pos_weight` "corrects" a
   blend of (a) genuine class prevalence inside campaigns and (b) which
   papers happened to be ingested — (b) is not a property of ignition
   physics.
2. **Single-class papers are trivially easy for a fingerprint model.**
   Identify the campaign → predict its constant label. In random splits these
   903 rows are essentially free accuracy; the LOPO audit confirms the
   single-class papers receive a mean predicted ignition probability of
   **0.92** even when held out (they are mostly standard-conditions ignition
   tests), so they also dilute pooled grouped metrics upward without testing
   any discrimination.
3. **Grouped fold variance.** With only 45 papers contributing both classes,
   a 5-fold grouped split has ~9 informative papers per test fold; one
   anomalous large paper (e.g. Rojas-Alva 2022, within-paper AUC 0.27) can
   move a fold's pooled AUC by several points. The ±0.07–0.10 fold spread of
   grouped CV is structural, which is why the Fable protocol repeats
   StratifiedGroupKFold over multiple shuffles and reports LOPO per-paper
   distributions instead of a single 5-fold mean.

## 3. Imbalance interaction: weighting must be combined

Paper weighting and class weighting interact: papers with many rows are
disproportionately the mixed (two-class) papers, so pure `1/N_paper`
weighting *up-weights* the single-class ignition-only papers (40 papers →
40/85 of the total weight for 22% of rows) and worsens effective class
imbalance from the classifier's viewpoint. This is why requirement B
(combined `paper_weight × class_weight`) is benchmarked rather than assumed:
the combination, not either component alone, controls both axes. See
`eval_outputs/comparison_table.md` for measured results of
none/inverse/sqrt/effective/log × none/balanced.

## 4. Recommendations

1. Report per-paper (LOPO) metric distributions, not only pooled means.
2. Use `1/sqrt(N_paper)` or effective-number weighting as the default
   compromise; pure `1/N` gives a 3-row paper the same total influence as a
   314-row campaign, which over-trusts tiny studies and amplifies the
   single-class skew (§3).
3. For future data ingestion, prioritise no-ignition-rich sources; the
   fastest scientific win available to this project is reducing the 40/0
   asymmetry of single-class papers.
